What is the role of a technical writer?

Clearly communicate and accurately explain complex information to a user.

We prioritize:

  • Simplicity
  • Clarity
  • Usability

Because:

  • It improves comprehension

Why should we write simply?

If you’ve read a page or a sentence and by the end, you have no idea what you just read and then you read it again and again until you kind of understand the context before moving on. There is a good chance that the information was both complex and not written simply…

The Forgetting Curve

The Forgetting Curve theory, introduced by Hermann Ebbinghaus, hypothesized that you forget:

  • 50% of all new information in a day
  • 90% of all new information within a week

  • 50% of all new information in a day and 90% of all new information within a week when no attempt to retain the information is made
  • Rapid loss of information early on - slows over time…
  • Things that affect the curve include difficulty of the learned material (e.g., how meaningful it is) and its representation. If you don’t use and apply the information - you are more likely to forget it. If you don’t use it…you lose it.

Cognitive Load Theory

The Cognitive Load Theory, introduced by John Sweller, hypothesized that the amount of working memory resources used.

Essentially, working memory is extremely limited and becomes easily overloaded when faced with complex, unnecessary, or poorly organized information.

What does this mean for us?

  • Our users aren’t likely to retain much of the information we provide in the guides.
  • They will likely return to the material, often, to reference the same information repeatedly.

Things to avoid…

  • High word count
  • Long sentences & paragraphs
  • Compound sentences
  • Redundant, unneeded, excessive, superfluous words

  • Word Soup: Jumbled, collection of words, that either has no meaning or the meaning is unclear.
  • Avoid long paragraphs and sentences, high word count, and fluffs.

Do this!

Target: 12th grade reading level

Focused and concise

  • Aim to write at a 12th-grade reading level (essentially guarentees that our readers with high school diplomas, GEDs, and college degrees will be able read and absorb content)
  • Focused and concise - this isn’t a short-story about user provisioning
  • Write the Docs has Principles for great content and they cover “Skimmable content”. This is very applicable to our docs so intentionally structure content to allow users to quickly identify the content that they need and skip over the content that they don’t
  • Consistent application of text decoration and formatting according to the style guide. If we use bolded words to indicate user action or quotes to indicate user input - then it allows our users to easily scan and identify procedures requiring user input
  • FOLLOW THE POSIT PPD STYLE GUIDE
